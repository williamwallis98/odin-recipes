Spaghetti with Meat Sauce

Description

Spaghetti has always been one of my favorite foods. Growing up we didn't have a ton of money and one really great thing about spaghetti is that you can pretty easily make a ton of it to feed a lot of people, even if you don't have a whole lot of money. If you can afford some ground beef to go into it though, preferablly a whole lot of ground beef, then it goes from being ehhh this is pretty good to being one of the best tasting dishes that I can think of.

Ingredients

  • 1lb Ground Beef
  • 1 Medium Onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 green bell pepper (diced)
  • 16oz tomato sauce
  • 6oz tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 16oz dried angel hair pasta

Equipment

  • Sauce pan
  • Larger pot for boiling noodles
  • Collander
  • Spatula

Directions

  1. Combine ground beef, onion, garlic, and green pepper in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook and stir until meat is browned and crumbly and vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain grease.
  2. Stir diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste into the pan. Season with oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer spaghetti sauce for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  3. As the sauce finishes up, boil angel hair pasta for roughly 5 minutes in salted water. After 5 minutes, drain and rinse pasta in a collander
