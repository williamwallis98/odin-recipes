Iced Coffee

Description

This is my go to coffee that i make every morning. I tend to not enjoy my coffee to be all that hot because it takes a while for it to cool down, and generally whenever i drink coffee i want it to be drinkable as fast as possible. This drink tastes especially good on hot summer days, although it can be refreshing pretty much any time of the year.

Credit for this recipe goes to James Hoffmann

Ingredients

  • 200g of ice
  • 300ml of water
  • 32.5g of whole bean coffee
  • 120ml of your favorite creamer

Equipment

  • Kitchen scale
  • a kettle or pot for boiling water
  • a brewer of some kind (Hario V60, French Press, Chemex, Aeropress, etc)

Directions

  1. Bring 300ml of water to a boil
  2. while the water is coming up to a boil, grind coffee beans to a medium fine consistancy
  3. tare your scale and place brewing vessel on top of the scale
  4. using brewing method of choice add 65ml of water to the ground coffee to create a bloom and swirl. Wait 30 seconds
  5. Add the remaining 235ml of water
  6. Once the coffee finishes brewing, pour the coffee over 200g of ice and swirl to melt the ice.
  7. Serve over ice
